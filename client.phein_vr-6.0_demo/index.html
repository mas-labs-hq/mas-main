Data Privacy & Security Policy

Your data protection is our priority

Local-First Architecture

Phein CV Screener operates entirely within your web browser using advanced client-side processing technology. All document parsing, candidate analysis, and data handling occurs locally on your device. No CV files, personal candidate information, or recruitment data is ever transmitted to external servers, cloud storage systems, or third-party services.

Data Retention & Control

  • All uploaded documents remain exclusively on your local machine and are automatically cleared when you close your browser session or refresh the application.
  • You retain complete ownership and control over all candidate data processed through this application at all times.
  • No analytics, tracking cookies, or telemetry data is collected from your screening activities or document contents.

Regulatory Compliance

This application is designed to support compliance with major data protection regulations including the Kenya Data Protection Act 2019, the European Union General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and other applicable privacy frameworks. The local-first processing model minimizes data transfer risks and supports your organization's data governance requirements.

MortApps Studios is committed to protecting your privacy and the confidentiality of candidate information.

Phein User Guide

Complete feature reference

Unlock Premium Version

Get unrestricted access to AI Detection Lens, Age Detection, Work Authorization Screening, unlimited document uploads, and full AI Skill Extraction. labs@mortappsstudios.com

1

Job Criteria

Start by entering the Job Role title (e.g. "Senior Software Engineer"). Then define your skill requirements in two categories:

  • Core Competencies (red tags) — Must-have skills. Candidates are scored primarily on these (60% weight). Type each skill and press Enter to add it.
  • Desirable Skills (green tags) — Nice-to-have skills. These add bonus points but are not required (40% weight).

AI Skill Extraction: Paste a full job description into the AI textarea and click "Extract Skills." Phein will auto-detect relevant skills from the JD and present them as clickable suggestions.

2

Candidates to Pick

Use the +/- controls to set how many top candidates Phein should display after screening (up to 5,500). This does not limit how many CVs you can upload, only how many ranked results are shown.

3

Screening Options

Toggle optional detection features before running your screening:

  • AI Detection Lens — When enabled, analyzes each candidate's CV text using 7 statistical signals (vocabulary bias, sentence burstiness, specificity, structural uniformity, transition density, pattern repetition, hedging language) to estimate the probability of AI-generated or AI-assisted content. Each candidate receives a percentage score and a classification label: Not AI Generated (green), Possibly AI Assisted (amber), Likely AI Generated (orange), or AI Generated (red). Click the tag for a detailed breakdown. This analysis is purely informational and does not affect the candidate's screening score.
  • Work Authorization Detection — Scans every CV for visa and work permit keywords (H-1B, Green Card, Tier 2, Work Permit, Right to Work, ILR, Sponsorship, EAD, etc.). Candidates are categorized as Authorized (citizen, PR, ILR) or Visa Required.
  • Salary Information — Extracts salary, compensation, and package mentions with currency amounts from candidate documents.
  • Show LinkedIn & Portfolio Links — When enabled, automatically extracts and displays LinkedIn profile URLs and portfolio/website links found in candidate documents. Toggling this off hides the links from candidate cards. Enabled by default.
4

Clear Workspace Before Adding New Candidates

Important: Before uploading a new batch of candidates, click the Clear button. You will be asked to choose: Clear Candidates Only (removes uploaded documents and results but keeps your job criteria and skills) or Clear Entire Workspace (removes everything for a completely fresh start). If you are screening a new role, use "Clear Entire Workspace." If you are screening a new batch for the same role, use "Clear Candidates Only" to save time re-entering criteria.

5

Upload Documents

Drag and drop PDF or TXT files — or entire folders — into the upload zone. Phein auto-detects three document types:

  • CV / Resume — Scored at 60% of the total match weight.
  • Cover Letter — Scored at 40% of the total match weight.

When multiple documents belong to the same candidate (same name), Phein automatically groups them and calculates a combined score. Each candidate card shows document type badges (CV, Resume, CL) and a score breakdown.

6

Run Screening

Click "Find Top Candidates" to analyze all uploaded documents against your criteria. Phein uses semantic skill matching (aliases, qualification hierarchy, experience parsing, proficiency detection) to score each candidate. Results are ranked by match score and displayed as cards.

7

Understanding Candidate Cards

Each candidate card displays:

  • Name + Document Badges — Shows which document types were found (CV, Resume, CL).
  • Score — Overall match percentage (0-100%). Gold/silver/bronze left-border for top 3.
  • Score Breakdown — Separate CV score and Cover Letter score when both exist.
  • Matched Skills (green pills) — Skills the candidate has that match your criteria.
  • Gaps (red pills) — Required skills the candidate is missing.
  • Contact Info — Email and phone extracted from documents.
8

Recruiter Tools (on each card)

Each candidate card has powerful recruiter tools:

  • Status Tags — Click the badge next to a candidate's name to cycle through recruitment stages: None (neutral), Shortlisted (passes screening), Interview (selected for interview), Hold (backup/waitlist), Offer (offer extended), Rejected (not suitable). Status tags are included in PDF and CSV exports.
  • Interview Questions — Expand to see AI-generated interview questions tailored to each candidate's skill gaps.
  • Add Note — Click to add freeform recruiter notes. Notes auto-save and are included in both PDF and CSV exports.
  • AI Detection Tag — When AI Detection Lens is enabled, each candidate card shows a percentage tag indicating the likelihood of AI-generated content. Click this tag to open a detailed analysis popup with a probability gauge, section breakdown, 7-axis radar chart, triggered indicators, evidence highlights, and recommended interview questions based on the detection results.
  • Comparison Checkbox — Check 2 candidates, then click the floating "Compare" button for a side-by-side analysis table with system recommendation.
9

Statistics Dashboard

A collapsible dashboard appears above your results showing: average score, score distribution histogram, top matched skills across all candidates, and most common skill gaps. Click the header to expand/collapse.

10

Export Results

Click the Export button in the header to choose:

  • Export PDF — Professional branded report with your agency name, all candidate scores, contact info, matched skills, gaps, status tags, notes, visa info, and salary data. Enter your company name in the dialog before downloading.
  • Export CSV — Spreadsheet-compatible file with all screening data (rank, name, score, email, phone, matched skills, gaps, status, notes, visa, salary) for further analysis in Excel or Google Sheets.

    AI Detection Lens Disclaimer

    AI Detection Lens analyzes statistical patterns in document structure, vocabulary choice, and writing style to estimate the probability of AI-generated or AI-assisted content. This analysis is purely informational and will not affect the candidate's screening score in any way. Results indicate probability based on pattern analysis, not definitive conclusions. Some candidates may naturally exhibit patterns that the system flags. We strongly recommend using these results as a conversation starting point during interviews, consider raising targeted questions that could conclusively assess the candidate's authentic experience and capabilities. This feature should be used as one of many assessment tools, not as a standalone decision maker.
